    Feature
    Online form v4
    Related stories
    n/a

    Changes

  • Changing request type to radio buttons instead of checkboxes
  • Removing creditor reference number from direct payment journey
  • Content changes
  • Removing rent free weeks from rent details
  • Adding postal route
  • Adding postal route

    • Hypothesis

  • We believe that by changing ‘missing rent’ to ‘rent arrears’ throughout the service landlords will understand that it refers to rent that the tenant hasn’t paid. We’ll know this when we have less queries about this term in user testing.
  • We believe that by asking the landlord how much rent is owed avoids any confusion over whether they include rent payments that will be due before the end of the tenancy agreement. We’ll know this has worked when landlords enter amounts just for previous months’ rent.
  • We believe that by asking the landlord if the tenant has paid any rent since they were in rent arrears (previous question) is clearer than asking if the tenant is still missing payments. We’ll know this when landlords can easily explain what the question means in user testing.
  • We believe that by changing ‘Tenant reference number’ to ‘Payment reference’ landlords will recognise this as just something that appears on their bank statement rather than being something DWP provides. We also believe that by changing the help text to explain that they choose the reference number will reassure them that it’s not something they need to get from the tenant or DWP. We’ll know this when they can explain what a ‘payment reference’ is in user testing.
  • We believe that by adding ‘(if known)’ next to creditor reference number will make it clear to landlords that it’s not something they need to give and that not having one won’t slow down their application. We also believe that adding more information about what a creditor reference is will reduce the amount of questions we get on this during user testing.
  • We believe that by adding information on what happens if the application is successful, the landlord will be less likely to contact DWP to query what happens next. We’ll know this when we ask the landlords what they would do next in user testing.

    Research done with private landlords

    Changes

  • Amending 'request reason' page to cover off tier 1 and tier 2 reasons
  • Removing mention of 8 weeks of arrears and changing to 2 months to align with policy and guidance
  • Removing details of missing payments: we only need to know the amount and the frequency.

    Hypothesis

    We've seen users struggling to add arrears details into the form. We believe that if we simplify the amount of information we are asking for, the case managers can still process the application resulting in a faster application process.

    Research notes used for iterations

    Research done with private landlords

  • Confusion around the creditor and tenant reference numbers. We were aware this may happen and hoping content design support will help to iron this out.
  • Users are struggling when entering missing payment information for a number of reasons. For example, missing out the 'add another' button. After more research, we do not need to collect all of this information so the questions have been changed to reflect this.

    Online form v2

    Changes

    After a meeting with UC47 SME's, we looked at items that are not used for APA purposes, and removed them:

  • Temporary accommodation
  • Service charge details
  • Joint tenancy and partner details
  • NINO
  • Adding more rent arrears options: missing payment 1, 2, 3 etc

    The length of the check your answers page was a concern, so in this prototype it is broken down into two, one after the initial data gather, and another after the rent arrears details.

    It was observed that there was a lot of "change" buttons on the check your answers page which essentially take the user to the same page. These have been removed to be one change page per section.

    Hypothesis

    We believe that by adding more 'missing payment' entry options, will allow the user to understand how this input section works. We'll know this when we see users entering information correctly.

    We believe that case managers do not need some of the information asked for on the APA, to process the APA itself, because we have seen them using this for other related housing work. So if we remove these, we'll see them being able to process the APA anyway.

    We believe that having the arrears question separate from the other reasons for application will improve the user experience, because most people are in 8 weeks of arrears and will not need to read all the additional information.

    Content changes - see documentation.

    Research notes used for iterations

    Research done with private landlords

  • Form relatively easy to fill in
  • Users struggled to see the "add another button on rent arrears details"
  • Users don't understand the meaning of 'reference number'
  • Private landlords have differing amounts of information about their tenants.
  • Confusion over language used in 'rent statement'
  • Confusion over service charges
  • Declaration unclear (needs work)
